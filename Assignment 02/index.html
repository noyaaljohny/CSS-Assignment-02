About Toblerone

Toblerone is a Swiss chocolate bar that is known for its distinctive triangular shape. It is made from milk chocolate and nougat with honey and almonds. Toblerone is produced by the Swiss chocolate maker Mondelēz International and is sold in over 120 countries around the world.

History

Toblerone was first created in 1908 by Theodor Tobler and his cousin Emil Baumann in Switzerland. The name "Toblerone" is derived from the Tobler and Torrone, which is Italian for nougat. The chocolate bar was an instant success and has remained a popular treat for over a century.

Flavors

Toblerone is available in several flavors, including:

  • Milk chocolate
  • Dark chocolate
  • White chocolate
  • Fruit and Nut
  • Almond
  • Honeycomb crisp
